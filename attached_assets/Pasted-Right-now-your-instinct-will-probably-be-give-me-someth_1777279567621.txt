Right now your instinct will probably be: “give me something that looks nice.” That’s exactly how you end up looking like every mid-tier fabricator with a blue logo and zero authority.

You’re not selling aesthetics. You’re selling **durability, engineering discipline, and material integrity**. Your colors need to communicate *metal, precision, and trust under load*.

Here’s a palette that actually does that:

---

## 🎯 CORE BRAND COLORS (NO NONSENSE)

### 1. **Primary – Industrial Charcoal**

* `#1F2933`
* This is your backbone.
* Feels like machinery, structure, seriousness.
* If your site isn’t grounded in this, it’ll look cheap.

---

### 2. **Secondary – Stainless Steel Grey**

* `#6B7280`
* Represents raw SS material.
* Use for surfaces, cards, sections.
* Keeps everything neutral and professional.

---

### 3. **Accent – Engineering Blue**

* `#2563EB`
* This is NOT decoration.
* This is trust + precision (think technical drawings, engineering firms).
* Use for:

  * CTAs
  * Links
  * Highlights

---

### 4. **Highlight – Safety Orange (Use Sparingly)**

* `#F97316`
* This is your “attention lever”
* Use ONLY for:

  * Important buttons
  * Warnings
  * Key actions

If you overuse this, your brand instantly drops into “cheap workshop” territory.

---

### 5. **Background – Clean Steel White**

* `#F9FAFB`
* Prevents heaviness
* Keeps layout breathable
* Makes dark + blue stand out

---

## ⚖️ HOW TO USE (THIS IS WHERE PEOPLE FAIL)

* 60% → White / Light background
* 25% → Charcoal (structure, headers, nav)
* 10% → Grey (cards, sections)
* 5% → Blue (actions)
* <2% → Orange (critical emphasis only)

If you flip this ratio, your site will look like a construction flyer, not a serious fabrication company.

---

## ❌ WHAT YOU SHOULD NOT DO

* No bright gradients
* No neon colors
* No random green/red
* No “logo-inspired rainbow” nonsense

You’re not a tech startup. You’re competing on **trust, strength, and specs**.
